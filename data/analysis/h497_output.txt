h497: GOLDEN Standard vs Hierarchy Holdout Validation
======================================================

OBJECTIVE: Test whether non-hierarchy GOLDEN predictions ("standard" rule: freq>=10 + mechanism + tier1)
have significantly lower holdout precision than hierarchy-based GOLDEN predictions.

FULL DATA BASELINE (455 diseases with GT + embeddings):
Total GOLDEN: 283
  standard: 55.7% (54/97, 34% of GOLDEN)
  hierarchy: 89.5% (153/171, 60% of GOLDEN)
  other: 73.3% (11/15, 5% of GOLDEN)

HOLDOUT EVALUATION (5-seed, 80/20 disease split):

Seed 42: GOLDEN 32.0% (8/25)
  standard: 0.0% (0/4)    ← small-n outlier
  hierarchy: 36.8% (7/19)
  other: 1/2

Seed 123: GOLDEN 72.2% (52/72)
  standard: 77.8% (28/36)
  hierarchy: 71.9% (23/32)
  other: 1/4

Seed 456: GOLDEN 78.2% (43/55)
  standard: 82.8% (24/29)
  hierarchy: 82.6% (19/23)
  other: 0/3

Seed 789: GOLDEN 68.6% (24/35)
  standard: 71.4% (20/28)
  hierarchy: 66.7% (4/6)
  other: 0/1

Seed 2024: GOLDEN 84.9% (45/53)
  standard: 78.8% (26/33)
  hierarchy: 93.3% (14/15)
  other: 5/5

AGGREGATE (5-seed mean ± std):
  Standard GOLDEN:  62.2% ± 31.3% (n/seed=26.0)
  Hierarchy GOLDEN: 70.3% ± 19.1% (n/seed=19.0)
  Difference (H-S): +8.1pp ± 18.0pp
  Paired t-stat: 1.01
  Significant (|t|>2.78)? NO (df=4, p>0.35)

EXCLUDING SEED 42 (small-n outlier, only 4 standard predictions):
  Standard: 77.7%
  Hierarchy: 78.6%
  Difference: +0.9pp (essentially equal)

DECISION: Standard GOLDEN holdout (62.2%) is ABOVE HIGH holdout avg (60.8% ± 7.2%),
and the gap vs hierarchy is NOT significant (p>0.35).

NO DEMOTION NEEDED. Standard GOLDEN predictions are justified as GOLDEN tier.

KEY INSIGHTS:
1. Full-data gap (standard 55.7% vs hierarchy 89.5%) is mostly overfitting in hierarchy
2. On holdout, both converge (62-78% range)
3. Seed 42 outlier (0/4 standard) drives the high variance (±31.3%)
4. With ≥26 standard predictions per seed, the standard rule is well-characterized
5. Standard GOLDEN represents diverse high-frequency drugs (corticosteroids, lidocaine,
   adalimumab, methotrexate) for tier-1 diseases — clinically appropriate
